How to become a Veterinarian

  1. Decide if Vet School is the right choice for you

    2. Vet school is a lot of work and time, it is also quit expensive. Before any other step you want to figure out if becoming a Veterinarian is really in your books.

  2. Complete Undergraduate Degree

    3. Most vet schools expect applicants to have at least completed a backelor's degree before applying. Click here to see the undergrads most schools look for. Some schools require additional courses, make sure you check the requirements for the shcool before applying.

  3. Gain Experience
    4. Intern at your local veterinary centers, humane socieites, or any other experiences having to do with animals
  4. Apply to Veterinary Schools

    5. Most schools ask for a personal statement, two or more recommendation letters, secondary essays, and resume. Each school is different, make sure to check what each school requires for application.

  5. Complete a DVM Degree

    6. Once into vet school, you need to complete a four-year Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM) degree. The first two years of vet school mainly focus on science courses with labs, the third year focuses on clinical experience. The final year mostly includes clinical rotations to attain hands-on experiences.

  6. Take the NAVLE

    7. Next up is The North American Veterinary Licensing Exam (NAVLE). This exam is multiple-choice and it is required for every U.S. veterinary licensure. Once this exam is passed, veterinary medicine can be practiced.

  7. Complete a Residency Program

    8. This step is optional but useful. If you want to specialize you can attend a residency program after completing vet school. Residency can help you obtain positions tailored to your interests and some with higher pay. You can see the different pay for differnet jobs here and here.

  8. Start up that Veterinary Career!

#2: Helping the Community

There are many animals in the Virgin Islands; especially strays. These animals need attention and there are very few veterinarians to help them. In the world as a whole there are more pets than vets. Vets help the communities pets stay safe and healthy. The Humane Society in St. Thomas harbors stray animals until they find a home. However, there are not enough workers and volunteers that add up to the amount of animals that live at the Humane Society.

By becoming a vet you can help the community as a whole. In due time this world is going to need more vets because the animal population keeps growing and it does not plan on stopping.

If you love science, why not be a vet. You get to practice science on a daily. There are so many jobs that have to do with animals and science. Its a 2 in 1 combo who doesn't love animals and science?
